Myths Regarding OTDA

Organ donation can be a scary concept to some people, and that is because there is a plethora of myths or things that people don't know/understand about the concept and process. Just below, there are some myths and Q/A-styled facts that may shed some light on the subject.


Will donating organs change my appearance?
Absolutely not. Doctors will remove the organs surgically and will maintain the utmost dignity and respect for the donor. There are so little alterations, in fact, that donation does not interfere with open casket funeral arrangements.
Does my religion support organ donation?
All recognized religions support organ and tissue donation, you're good to go!

There are plenty of organ donors already, they don't need me.
Absolutely false. Nationally, there are 123,000+ patients waiting for life-saving organ transplants. There are over 6,400 waiting in the Gift of Life service area alone.

Only the wealthy and famous recieve organs.
False. Most major insurances cover heart, liver, kidney, and lung transplants. Donors don't need to pay to donate their organs, either.

I have a history of medical illness, are my organs still donateable?
When one dies, Gift of Life staff will look at the person's medical and social histories to determine if they are suitable for donation. There are a few diseases that can prevent someone from being a donor, cancer and HIV/AIDS are among them.

Isn't organ donation still experimental?
No, organ donation has been a successful life-saving procedure for over 50 years.

Am I too old to be an organ donor?
Never. There is no set age limit for an organ donor. As long as your organs are functioning and suitable for donation, it doesn't matter how old you are. Therefore, people of any age can feel free to register as an organ donor.
